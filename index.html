Introduction

Cookies are small files that websites place on a user's device. They can help save settings, remember logins, and support website features. Privacy is connected to cookies because some of them collect browsing data and track activity across websites.

Key Terms

  • Cookie: a small file stored by a web browser.
  • Session cookie: a cookie that usually disappears when the browser closes.
  • Persistent cookie: a cookie that stays on a device for a longer period.
  • Third-party cookie: a cookie placed by another service, often for ads or tracking.
  • Consent: permission a user gives before some data is collected.

Why This Topic Matters

Convenience

Cookies can improve the user experience by remembering preferences and keeping users signed in.

Tracking

Some cookies follow user behavior for advertising and analytics. This can raise privacy concerns.

Control

Users can often review browser settings and cookie consent choices to manage what data is collected.

Basic Facts

  • Cookies are stored by web browsers.
  • Some cookies are required for websites to function.
  • Other cookies are used for tracking and marketing.
  • Users can often manage cookies in browser settings.
